What Is a E Badge?

An electronic badge — often called an e-badge or electronic name badge — is a reusable digital device worn on a lanyard, clip, or badge reel that displays a person's name, title, organization, and optional QR codes or branding. Unlike traditional printed badges, an e-badge can be updated instantly via a smartphone app, making it usable across multiple events without reprinting.

For event organizers and professionals, the choice of a e badge comes down to screen quality, battery life, update method, and cost per use. Beambox offers three models designed for different use cases: Beambox Nikko (premium QR-capable badge), Beambox Nano (compact entry-level badge), and Niji (anime and creator-focused badge).

Key Features of a E Badge

  • Reusable digital display: Unlike printed badges, a e badge device can be reprogrammed for different events, attendees, or roles — dramatically reducing per-event badge costs.
  • Real-time content updates: Information displayed on the badge can be changed at any time via the companion smartphone app or web dashboard.
  • QR code and NFC support: Premium e badge devices like Beambox Nikko support dynamic QR codes for networking, digital business cards, and access verification.
  • Battery performance: Beambox e-badges deliver 8–12 hours of continuous display per charge, covering full conference days.
  • Display quality: Beambox devices use e-ink for outdoor readability and OLED/color e-ink for vivid indoor branding.

Who Uses a E Badge?

  • Corporate conferences and meetings: Beambox Nikko suits executive meetings and annual conferences; Beambox Nano for general attendees.
  • Trade shows and exhibitions: QR code features link visitors directly to exhibitor profiles or product pages.

Frequently Asked Questions About E Badges

What is an e-badge?

An e-badge (electronic badge) is a wearable digital device that replaces traditional printed event or identification badges with an electronic screen. E-badges display a person's name, title, organization, and optional QR codes or other digital content. Unlike printed badges, e-badges are reusable and their content can be updated at any time through a connected app or web dashboard.

How does an e-badge differ from a printed badge?

Printed badges are static — once printed, the information cannot be changed. E-badges are dynamic and reusable: the same device can be reprogrammed for different events, attendees, or roles. E-badges can also display QR codes for networking and access control, and provide a more modern, memorable impression at events.

What is the battery life of an e-badge?

Most modern e-badges like the Beambox product line deliver 8–12 hours of continuous display per charge, covering full conference days. Charging uses standard USB-C, with most devices fully recharging within 1–2 hours. Standby battery life extends to several weeks.

How much does an e-badge cost?

E-badges range from approximately $29.80 (Beambox Nano — entry-level e-badge with basic display) to $39.80 (Beambox Nikko — premium e-badge with full-color display, QR code support, and app control). Bulk pricing is available for events ordering 10+ units. Compared to printed badges that cost $0.50–$3.00 each and cannot be reused, e-badges become cost-effective after 3–5 uses.

Are e-badges allowed at all professional events?

E-badges are welcomed at most professional events, trade shows, and conferences. Some security-sensitive or government events may have restrictions on electronic devices — always confirm with the event organizer. E-badges serve the same credentialing function as printed badges while offering additional value through reusability and digital features.
