What Is a Led Badge?

An electronic badge — often called an e-badge or electronic name badge — is a reusable digital device worn on a lanyard, clip, or badge reel that displays a person's name, title, organization, and optional QR codes or branding. Unlike traditional printed badges, an e-badge can be updated instantly via a smartphone app, making it usable across multiple events without reprinting.

For event organizers and professionals, the choice of a LED badge comes down to screen quality, battery life, update method, and cost per use. Beambox offers three models designed for different use cases: Beambox Nikko (premium QR-capable badge), Beambox Nano (compact entry-level badge), and Niji (anime and creator-focused badge).

Key Features of a Led Badge

  • Reusable digital display: Unlike printed badges, a LED badge device can be reprogrammed for different events, attendees, or roles — dramatically reducing per-event badge costs.
  • Real-time content updates: Information displayed on the badge can be changed at any time via the companion smartphone app or web dashboard.
  • QR code and NFC support: Premium LED badge devices like Beambox Nikko support dynamic QR codes for networking, digital business cards, and access verification.
  • Battery performance: Beambox e-badges deliver 8–12 hours of continuous display per charge, covering full conference days.
  • Display quality: Beambox devices use e-ink for outdoor readability and OLED/color e-ink for vivid indoor branding.

Who Uses a Led Badge?

  • Corporate conferences and meetings: Beambox Nikko suits executive meetings and annual conferences; Beambox Nano for general attendees.
  • Trade shows and exhibitions: QR code features link visitors directly to exhibitor profiles or product pages.
  • Networking events: E-badges with QR codes replace traditional business cards — attendees scan to access digital business cards instantly.

Frequently Asked Questions About Led Badges

What is an LED badge?

An LED badge is a wearable electronic device with a screen — typically e-ink, LCD, or OLED — that displays a person's name, role, or other information. LED badges are designed to replace traditional printed paper or plastic name badges with a reusable, updatable digital alternative.

How bright are LED badges for indoor and outdoor events?

E-ink LED badges like Beambox are readable in direct sunlight, making them ideal for outdoor events. OLED and LCD LED badges offer vibrant color but perform best indoors or in shaded areas. Beambox uses e-ink technology for maximum outdoor readability.

How long does the battery last on an LED badge?

Beambox LED badges deliver 8–12 hours of continuous display on a single USB-C charge, sufficient for full conference days. Standby time extends to several weeks. Charging takes approximately 1–2 hours.

Can LED badges display logos and images?

Yes — Beambox Nikko and Beambox Niji support full-color image and logo display, making them suitable for organizations that want brand-consistent credentials. Logos appear clearly on the e-ink screen.

Are LED badges allowed at all events?

Most events welcome LED badges as they serve the same credentialing purpose as printed badges. Some security-sensitive events may have restrictions on electronic devices — check with event organizers. Beambox badges have no external radio transmission and use only Bluetooth for local sync.
