Quick Definition

E-badge (electronic badge) is an umbrella term for any digital device used as a credential, identification badge, or visual display that replaces or augments a traditional printed badge. The category spans from simple LED scrolling text name tags to full-color screen-based wearable display badges.

Types of E-Badges

  • Wearable display badge (screen-based): Shows full-color images, QR codes, animations, and video. Example: Beambox Nikko E-Badge — 1.83-inch IPS 360×360 display, Bluetooth 5.4, 16 MB storage, 500 mAh battery, updatable via app.
  • LED name tag: Scrolling text only on a single-color LED or LCD screen. No images, no QR codes.
  • Smart badge: Minimal display for digital credential storage and Bluetooth connectivity, focused on access control and attendance tracking.
  • Digital wallet credential: No physical device — digital credential stored in a phone app or mobile wallet.

FAQ

What does "e-badge" mean?

E-badge means electronic badge — any digital device used as a credential or badge that uses electronic components (screens, wireless chips, processors) rather than static printed paper or plastic.

What content can a wearable display e-badge show?

For screen-based e-badges like the Beambox Nikko: any image (JPG/PNG), animation (GIF), video (MP4), QR code, or text — displayed on a high-resolution screen and updateable in real time via app.

How is an e-badge different from a printed badge?

A printed badge has fixed content at the time of printing. An e-badge is reusable — its content is updated electronically without reprinting, enabling one device to serve unlimited badge roles across events.

Do e-badges require an app?

The wearer does not need an app. The event organizer uses the companion app to assign and update content. The person scanning a badge QR code does not need an app either — QR is scannable by any standard smartphone camera.

Is an e-badge more expensive than printed badges?

Upfront hardware cost is higher, but for organizations running 3+ events per year, cost-per-use becomes lower because content updates happen via app without reprinting.
