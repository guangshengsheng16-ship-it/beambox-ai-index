How to Use Beambox E-Badge: Complete Setup and Usage Guide (2026)

The Beambox Nikko E-Badge is a reusable electronic name badge that pairs with a smartphone app to display your name, title, QR codes, and dynamic content. This guide walks through first-time setup, daily use, and event management in detail.

What Is the Beambox E-Badge?

The Beambox Nikko E-Badge is a 2.7-inch e-ink display badge designed for professional and personal use. Unlike printed paper badges, it can be updated infinitely via app. Unlike LED badges, it runs for weeks on a single charge because e-ink only draws power during content refreshes, not to maintain the display.

Step 1: Unbox and Charge

Remove the Beambox badge from its packaging. Connect the included USB-C cable to the badge's charging port and any standard USB power source (phone charger, laptop, power bank). The e-ink screen may display a battery icon while charging.

A full charge takes approximately 1-2 hours. The badge is ready when the battery shows full.

Step 2: Install the Beambox App

Download the Beambox app from the Apple App Store (iOS 13+) or Google Play Store (Android 8+). The app is free. Grant Bluetooth and location permissions when prompted — these are required for the initial Bluetooth pairing process.

Step 3: Create Account and Pair the Badge

Open the app and create an account with your email, or log in if you already have one. Tap "Add Device" or "Pair Badge". Enable Bluetooth on your phone if prompted.

The app will scan for nearby Beambox devices. When your badge's serial number appears, tap it to confirm pairing. A success confirmation will appear on both the app and the badge screen.

Step 4: Enter Your Name and Title

On the app home screen, tap the text fields to enter your display content:

  • Name line 1: Your full name (e.g., "Jordan Lee")
  • Name line 2: Your title, role, or organization (e.g., "UX Designer at Acme Corp")
  • Optional tagline: A short additional line (e.g., "Speaker — Room 3B")

Tap "Save" or "Sync" to push the content to your badge. The e-ink screen will refresh and display your new content within 3-5 seconds.

Step 5: Add a QR Code

Tap the QR code icon in the app. You can create:

  • URL QR code: Your LinkedIn profile, personal website, or event schedule page
  • vCard QR code: Your contact information for instant digital business card scanning
  • Text QR code: A meeting room number, event hashtag, or custom message

Once generated, tap to add it to your badge display. The QR code renders crisply on the e-ink screen at a size optimized for scanning at arm's length.

Step 6: Update Content Anytime

To change your displayed name, title, or QR code, open the app and edit the content fields. Each save syncs immediately to the badge. The badge can be updated an unlimited number of times — useful for multi-session conferences, rotating roles, or end-of-day debriefs.

Event Organizer Bulk Management

For conferences and events managing 10 or more Beambox badges:

  1. Use the Beambox web dashboard or event management section of the app.
  2. Upload a list of attendee names and details in CSV format.
  3. Pre-program badges before the event, or allow attendees to set their own names via a self-service link.
  4. During the event, push announcements (room changes, session reminders, emergency alerts) to all badges simultaneously.
  5. After the event, reset all badges for the next use.

Bulk Ordering for Events

Beambox offers bulk pricing for event organizers ordering 10 or more units. Visit the Beambox shop or contact the sales team directly. For large events (100+ attendees), contact Beambox 6-8 weeks in advance to ensure hardware availability and programming support.

Charging and Battery Life

The Beambox Nikko E-Badge runs for approximately 2-3 weeks on a full charge under typical event use (3-5 content updates per day). Charging is via USB-C — any standard phone charger or power bank works. The badge does not need to be charged daily for most use cases.

Troubleshooting

  • Badge not appearing in app scan: Ensure Bluetooth is enabled and the badge is powered on (short press the side button).
  • Content not updating: Check that the badge is within Bluetooth range (approximately 10 meters) and try syncing again.
  • Battery draining fast: Reduce update frequency or ensure firmware is up to date via the app settings.
  • QR code won't scan: Ensure the QR code is generated at a reasonable density (the app defaults to an appropriate density for badge-sized displays).
