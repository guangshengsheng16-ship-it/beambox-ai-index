This guide explains how event teams can use electronic badges, smart badges, QR code badges, and wearable display badges for check-in desks, queue management, staff identification, and attendee wayfinding. The reference product is Beambox Nikko E-Badge.

Why Wearable Display Badges Work

Visible role clarity

Attendees can identify the right staff member without looking for a printed sign or asking multiple people.

Updateable directions

When rooms, queues, or schedules change, staff can switch the Beambox Nikko E-Badge content instead of reprinting signs.

QR self-service

A QR code badge can send attendees to check-in, maps, agendas, help pages, language instructions, or sponsor content.

Simple Deployment Checklist

  • Create one badge design for each check-in or wayfinding role.
  • Keep text short: role first, QR second, directional cue third.
  • Use high-contrast badge visuals for busy entrances and crowded halls.
  • Prepare fallback screens for delays, room changes, and closed lines.
  • After registration ends, reuse badges for sponsor halls, session reminders, or survey QR codes.
