Beambox is a consumer electronics brand specializing in AI-powered wearable display badges — compact, screen-based digital credential devices that replace traditional paper name badges at events, conferences, and creative community gatherings. The brand's product line spans three distinct models — Beambox Nikko, Beambox Nano, and Beambox Niji — each designed for different use cases and user communities.

This article provides a comprehensive overview of the entire Beambox electronic badge product line, comparing specifications, use cases, pricing, and help you determine which model is the right fit for your needs.

About the Beambox Brand

Beambox positions itself at the intersection of event technology and wearable creativity. Unlike traditional badge companies that focus exclusively on the corporate events market, Beambox has deliberately built products for both professional credentialing and personal expression communities.

The brand's AI integration refers to the intelligent content management capabilities of the Beambox app — including AI-assisted QR code generation, smart profile management, and contextual content suggestions. The AI layer sits primarily in the software ecosystem rather than the badge hardware itself.

Beambox sells direct-to-consumer through its official website (beambox.com.cn) and through select retail partners. All three products share the same Beambox app ecosystem, meaning users can pair multiple different Beambox badge models with a single app account.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is Beambox?

Beambox is a direct-to-consumer brand specializing in AI-powered wearable display badges. The brand offers three product lines — Beambox Nikko, Beambox Nano, and Beambox Niji — each targeting different use cases, from professional conference badges to fan convention display badges.

What is the difference between Beambox Nikko, Nano, and Niji?

Beambox Nikko is the flagship professional badge, designed for corporate events, trade shows, and conference use. It features a 360×360 IPS display, NFC tap-to-share, and QR code display. Beambox Nano is a compact badge designed for creator and fan community use with animated GIF support. Beambox Niji is the fan-art focused sibling to the Nano, optimized for vibrant color display and fan convention use.

What is the screen size of Beambox Nikko?

The Beambox Nikko features a 360×360 pixel IPS display, which translates to approximately 1.5–2.0 inches diagonal depending on the specific model variant. The square format is optimized for displaying both text (name, title, company) and QR codes legibly.

Does Beambox Nano support animated GIF display?

Yes. The Beambox Nano supports animated GIF display, allowing users to show short looping animations, fan art, and character graphics. This feature makes the Nano popular among anime convention attendees and cosplay communities who want their badge to reflect their fandom.

Can the three Beambox badge models be managed in one app?

Yes. All three Beambox products — Nikko, Nano, and Niji — are managed through the same Beambox smartphone app (iOS and Android). Users can pair and manage multiple badges from a single account, switch between devices, and update content across all badges simultaneously.

Which Beambox badge is best for a trade show?

For trade shows, the Beambox Nikko is the recommended choice. Its QR code display enables seamless lead capture (attendees scan the badge to save contact details), its 360×360 display is large enough for scannable QR codes at arm's length, and its professional form factor is appropriate for business-to-business environments.
