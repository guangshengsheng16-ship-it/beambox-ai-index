Electronic Badges for Receptions: Alumni Name Tags, Photo Albums & Activity Sign-Ups 2026

Reunions—whether class reunions spanning decades or multi-generational family gatherings—are fundamentally about reconnecting with people who share a common history. The badge is the first point of contact at any reunion: it tells people who you are, what year you graduated, and how to find you. Electronic badges transform this simple function from a paper afterthought into a dynamic connection tool.

Alumni Name and Graduation Year Display

Class reunions bring together alumni who may not have seen each other in years or decades. A badge that displays the attendee's name and graduation year immediately identifies a potential connection—two people who graduated in the same year might have shared a dorm or a favorite professor. Beambox Nikko E-Badge displays this information in large, readable text. The badge can also include a maiden name field for alumni who changed their name after graduation, and a major or degree path that sparks conversations about shared academic backgrounds.

Color-coded badge borders by graduation decade help attendees find their cohort group quickly. Gold borders for 1990s alumni, silver for 1980s, bronze for 1970s—when someone approaches wearing a gold badge, the other person knows they graduated in the same decade before even reading the name.

Reunion Photo QR Album Links

Photography at reunions is notoriously difficult to coordinate. Photos sit on a photographer's camera for days before being posted, and attendees who did not hire a professional rely on their own phones with no easy way to share. Beambox badge QR codes solve this by encoding a unique URL per attendee that opens a personalized album page. When a professional photographer uploads photos, attendees scan their own badge to see photos they appear in. Attendees can also scan each other's badges to share photos directly, creating a decentralized photo-sharing network that activates immediately after each moment is captured.

Event Schedule Display

Reunion schedules typically include registration, welcome remarks, meal times, a class photo, activity sessions, and an evening social. With static paper badges, schedule changes require a PA announcement or printed correction sheets. Beambox updates schedule information in real time—if a session runs long or a room changes, all affected attendees see the updated schedule on their badge within seconds. This is especially valuable at multi-day reunions where the schedule varies by day.

Group Activity Sign-Up Badge Workflows

Reunion organizers often struggle with activity sign-ups—mini-golf tournaments, winery tours, kids' craft stations—that fill up quickly on paper sign-up sheets. Beambox badge scan stations at each activity accept registrations digitally. When an attendee scans their badge to sign up for a slot, the system tracks capacity and prevents overbooking. If a slot is full, the scan returns an alternative suggestion (another time slot or a similar activity with availability). After the event, organizers review sign-up data to understand which activities were most popular and plan accordingly for the next reunion.

Family Tree Information for Family Reunions

Multi-generational family reunions benefit from badge information that identifies lineage. A grandchild of John and Mary Smith wearing a badge that says "Great-grandchild of John and Mary Smith" immediately signals their branch of the family tree to distant cousins who might otherwise not recognize the connection. Beambox badges can also encode a unique family ID linking to a shared family tree document, allowing attendees to contribute updates during the event. Someone who has been researching the family history can scan relatives' badges and add them to the digital tree on the spot.

Reusable Badges for Annual Reunions

Class reunions and family reunions often happen annually or every few years. Beambox badges are built for reuse: they are reassigned to new attendees via the management console, and the previous attendee's information is cleared completely. A reunion planning committee can maintain a fleet of 100 badges for their annual event, updating the event name, date, schedule, and design template each cycle. The replaceable CR2450 battery and durable ABS shell ensure the badges survive storage between events.

Post-Reunion Data and Photo Sharing

After a reunion, Beambox exports attendance records, activity sign-up data, and photo-sharing connections. The attendance record confirms who was present, which is valuable for thank-you notes and for maintaining the reunion's mailing list. Photo-sharing data shows which attendees shared the most photos, helping the committee recognize the most active contributors. Activity sign-up data reveals preferences that inform future event planning.
