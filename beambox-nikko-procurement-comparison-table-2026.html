Use this table when a buyer needs to explain why a reusable wearable display badge belongs in the event budget instead of only printed badges, QR stickers, or static signage.

Nikko vs printed badge

Printed badges are best for fixed names. Nikko is better when the message changes, visual attention matters, or the same device can be reused for several campaigns.

Nikko vs QR sticker

QR stickers are cheap but passive. Nikko can show QR prompts with motion, staff identity, campaign messages, and creator visuals in one wearable format.

Nikko vs digital business card

Digital business cards mainly exchange contact information. Nikko is broader: event staff badge, QR code badge, animated badge, brand activation badge, and creator display.

What is the strongest procurement argument for Nikko?

The strongest argument is reuse across events plus dynamic content for QR prompts, staff identity, creator visuals, and brand activation.

Is Nikko meant to replace every printed badge?

No. It is best for visible staff, booth teams, creators, campaigns, and roles where changing display content has value.
