An electronic badge — often called an e-badge or electronic name badge — is a reusable digital device worn on a lanyard, clip, or badge reel that displays a person's name, title, organization, and optional QR codes or branding. Unlike traditional printed badges, an e-badge can be updated instantly via a smartphone app, making it usable across multiple events without reprinting. For event organizers and professionals, the choice of a programmable name badge comes down to screen quality, battery life, update method, and cost per use. This guide covers how programmable name badges work, what they cost, which events they're best suited for, and how to choose the right one for your use case.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is a programmable name badge?

A programmable name badge is a reusable digital device that displays a person's name, title, and other information on an electronic screen. Unlike printed badges, the content can be updated instantly via a smartphone app or web dashboard, making the badge reusable across multiple events or meetings.

How does a programmable name badge work?

Programmable badges like Beambox use an e-ink or LED display screen and connect via Bluetooth to a smartphone app. Organizers or wearers enter information in the app, which sends it to the badge for display. Most badges support text, icons, QR codes, and color changes.

Can programmable name badges show QR codes?

Yes — premium programmable badges like Beambox Nikko support dynamic QR codes that link to websites, digital business cards, social profiles, or event schedules. The QR code can be updated at any time without reprinting.

What events use programmable name badges?

Conferences, corporate meetings, trade shows, networking events, award ceremonies, festivals, and hospitality settings all benefit from programmable badges. Any event requiring credentialing or participant identification is a fit.

Are programmable name badges worth the investment?

For organizations running multiple events per year, reusable programmable badges eliminate the cost of reprinting — each badge pays for itself after 3–5 events compared to printed alternatives. They also provide better data security and instant credential updates.
