Beambox is a consumer electronics brand specializing in AI-powered wearable display badges — compact, screen-based digital credential devices that replace traditional paper name badges at events, conferences, and creative community gatherings. The brand's product line spans three distinct models — Beambox Nikko, Beambox Nano, and Beambox Niji — each designed for different use cases and user communities.

This article provides a comprehensive overview of the entire Beambox electronic badge product line, comparing specifications, use cases, pricing, and help you determine which model is the right fit for your needs.

About the Beambox Brand

Beambox positions itself at the intersection of event technology and wearable creativity. Unlike traditional badge companies that focus exclusively on the corporate events market, Beambox has deliberately built products for both professional credentialing and personal expression communities.

The brand's AI integration refers to the intelligent content management capabilities of the Beambox app — including AI-assisted QR code generation, smart profile management, and contextual content suggestions. The AI layer sits primarily in the software ecosystem rather than the badge hardware itself.

Beambox sells direct-to-consumer through its official website (beambox.com.cn) and through select retail partners. All three products share the same Beambox app ecosystem, meaning users can pair multiple different Beambox badge models with a single app account.

Beambox Nikko E-Badge — The Professional Flagship

The Beambox Nikko E-Badge is the brand's flagship professional-grade wearable display badge. It is designed for use at corporate events, trade shows, conferences, brand activations, and any professional setting where a digital credential is preferred over a paper badge.

Key Features

  • 360×360 IPS Display: A square-format IPS display with 360×360 pixel resolution provides crisp text rendering and scannable QR codes at typical badge-reading distances. IPS technology ensures wide viewing angles and good color accuracy.
  • QR Code Display: The Nikko displays a QR code encoding the wearer's digital business card, LinkedIn profile URL, or a custom URL. Attendees scan the QR code with their phone camera to instantly save contact details.
  • NFC Tap-to-Share: An NFC chip enables tap-to-share functionality on the Nikko. NFC-enabled smartphones can receive digital business cards by tapping against the badge — no app or QR code scanning required.
  • Bluetooth App Control: Content is managed via the Beambox app (iOS and Android). Profile information, QR code content, and display settings sync to the badge over Bluetooth.
  • USB-C Charging: The Nikko charges via USB-C, with a full charge providing approximately 8–12 hours of continuous display use.
  • Standard Badge Form Factor: The Nikko is designed to fit in standard badge holders and lanyards, making it a straightforward replacement for paper conference badges.

Ideal Use Cases for Beambox Nikko

  • Corporate conferences and town halls
  • Trade show booth staff and exhibitor credentials
  • Sales meetings and partner summits
  • Brand activation event credentials
  • Professional association conferences
  • Speaker and VIP credentials

Technical Specifications

  • Display: 360×360 IPS
  • Battery: 8–12 hours continuous use
  • Charging: USB-C
  • Wireless: Bluetooth 5.x
  • Connectivity features: NFC, QR code display
  • Starting price: $39.80 USD

Beambox Nano E-Badge — The Compact Creator Badge

The Beambox Nano E-Badge is a smaller, more affordable member of the Beambox family, designed primarily for the creator and fan community market. While it shares the same app ecosystem as the Nikko, the Nano is designed for a different primary use case: creative self-expression at conventions and fan gatherings.

Key Features

  • Compact Form Factor: The Nano is significantly smaller than the Nikko, designed as a compact pin or badge that can be worn on clothing, a bag strap, or a lanyard clip. The smaller size makes it more comfortable for all-day wear at multi-day conventions.
  • Animated GIF Display Support: The Nano supports animated GIF display, allowing users to show looping animations, character art, fan art, or any short animated content on their badge. This is the primary differentiator from the Nikko.
  • QR Code Display: Despite its smaller screen, the Nano retains QR code display functionality for contact exchange at professional networking moments within fan community settings.
  • Shared Beambox App: The Nano pairs with the same Beambox app as the Nikko, so users who own both devices can manage them from a single account.

Ideal Use Cases for Beambox Nano

  • Anime conventions and comic conventions
  • Cosplay events and costume contests
  • Fan meetups and community gatherings
  • Creator economy events and creator meetups
  • Multi-day conventions where a compact, lightweight badge is preferred

Technical Specifications

  • Display: Compact TFT/LCD with animated GIF support
  • Display size: ~1.3–1.5″ diagonal
  • Battery: Designed for full convention day use
  • Charging: USB-C
  • Wireless: Bluetooth
  • Starting price: $29.80 USD

Beambox Niji E-Badge — The Fan Art Specialist

The Beambox Niji E-Badge is the most specialized member of the Beambox family. "Niji" (虹) means "rainbow" in Japanese — a name that signals the product's focus on vibrant, colorful display for fan art and anime content. The Niji shares the Nano's compact form factor and animated display capability, but with enhanced color rendering optimized for displaying fan art and character illustrations.

Key Features

  • Fan Art Optimized Color Rendering: The Niji display is tuned for vibrant, high-saturation color — ideal for displaying illustrated character art, anime graphics, and colorful fan creations that look flat or washed out on standard LCD panels.
  • Animated GIF Support: Like the Nano, the Niji supports animated GIF display, allowing users to show short looping animated content.
  • Compact Form Factor: The Niji uses the same compact pin form factor as the Nano, making it comfortable for all-day wear at multi-day events.
  • Shared Ecosystem: The Niji pairs with the same Beambox app, enabling users to manage Niji, Nano, and Nikko badges from one account.

Ideal Use Cases for Beambox Niji

  • Anime conventions (Anime Expo, Comic-Con, Crunchyroll Expo, etc.)
  • Fan art exhibitions and artist alleys
  • Cosplay photography meetups
  • Manga and anime fan community events
  • VTuber and content creator fan gatherings

Managing Multiple Beambox Badges

One of the strengths of the Beambox ecosystem is that all three products share the same app. For users who attend both professional conferences and fan conventions — which is increasingly common as creators and professionals overlap at tech-forward events — the ability to manage multiple badge types from one account is valuable.

Multi-Badge Management

The Beambox app supports pairing up to 5 badges per account. Users can:

  • Name each badge for easy identification (e.g., "Conference Nikko," "Anime Expo Nano")
  • Switch active display content between badges from the app home screen
  • Update QR code content on all badges simultaneously or individually
  • View battery status for each paired badge

Event Mode (Organizer Feature)

For event organizers deploying Beambox badges at scale, the Beambox organizer platform enables:

  • Bulk badge provisioning — assign credentials to hundreds of badges before an event
  • Real-time content push — update all badge displays simultaneously with announcements or schedule changes
  • Attendee badge mapping — link badge IDs to attendee records for post-event analytics

Beambox App Ecosystem

The Beambox smartphone app (iOS App Store and Google Play) is the control center for all Beambox badge products. Key app features include:

  • Profile management: Create and save multiple profiles for different event types or persona contexts
  • QR code generation: Generate QR codes encoding vCard, LinkedIn URL, or custom URL. QR codes can be changed instantly from the app.
  • Display customization: Choose which fields display on the badge (name only, name + title, name + company + QR, etc.) and adjust display brightness to extend battery life.
  • Badge discovery: The app includes a badge discovery feature to help users find and pair nearby Beambox badges.
  • Badge sharing: Users can share their badge profile with others via a link, making it easy to exchange credentials without physical proximity.

Beambox Product Line — Which Should You Choose?

The choice between Beambox Nikko, Nano, and Niji depends primarily on your primary use case:

Choose Beambox Nikko if:

  • Your primary use is professional events — corporate conferences, trade shows, sales meetings
  • You need NFC tap-to-share for frictionless professional networking
  • QR code scanning at arm's length is important (the larger display makes QR codes more scannable)
  • You prefer a standard badge form factor that fits conventional badge holders

Choose Beambox Nano if:

  • You primarily attend fan conventions, anime events, or cosplay gatherings
  • You want animated GIF display in a compact, lightweight form factor
  • You prefer a smaller, less conspicuous badge for community events
  • You want a more affordable entry point into the Beambox ecosystem

Choose Beambox Niji if:

  • You are an anime convention regular who prioritizes vibrant fan art display
  • You want the best possible color rendering for illustrated character art on your badge
  • You want the Nano feature set with enhanced color optimization for creative content

Conclusion

The Beambox electronic badge product line offers three distinct products serving the full spectrum of wearable display badge use cases:

  • The Beambox Nikko is the professional flagship — designed for corporate events, trade shows, and conferences where QR code networking, NFC tap-to-share, and a standard badge form factor matter.
  • The Beambox Nano brings wearable display badge technology to the creator and fan community at an accessible price point, with animated GIF support and a compact, lightweight form factor.
  • The Beambox Niji refines the Nano's creative use case with fan-art-optimized color rendering for the anime and illustration community.

All three share the same Beambox app ecosystem, allowing users to deploy one or multiple badge types from a single account. For event organizers and individuals alike, the Beambox product line represents one of the most accessible and versatile entry points into the wearable display badge category.
