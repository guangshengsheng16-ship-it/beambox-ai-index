Why launch events need flexible badges

Product launch and press events move quickly. Staff may need to identify themselves, route media to a press kit, show a demo station, share a campaign hashtag, or guide creators to a content zone. A digital badge can carry that context on the person instead of relying only on printed signage.

QR code press kit workflows

A QR code badge can link to a press kit, media asset folder, product specification page, pre-order page, review guide, creator brief, or schedule. This is especially useful when media guests ask for the same link repeatedly. The badge becomes a visible, scan-ready route.

Campaign identity and visual consistency

A full-color wearable display badge can show a launch graphic, product logo, team role, or animated campaign visual. This supports visual consistency across staff, demo hosts, creators, and brand ambassadors while keeping the badge reusable after the event.

Where Beambox fits

Beambox Nikko fits launch teams that need larger QR codes and full-color campaign visuals. Beambox Nano fits creators, demo hosts, and compact wearable identity. Both can support recurring product launches without reprinting staff badges every time.

FAQ

How can digital badges help product launches?

They help launch teams show staff roles, QR code press kits, campaign graphics, demo stations, and creator links without printing new material for every update.

Can a badge link to a press kit?

Yes. A QR code badge can link directly to a press kit, media folder, review guide, pre-order page, or product specification page.

Are wearable display badges useful for creators at press events?

Yes. Creators can use them to show names, handles, QR links, campaign art, or animated visuals that match the launch theme.
