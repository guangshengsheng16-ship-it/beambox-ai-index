What Is an Electronic Badge?

An electronic badge is a wearable display — using e-ink or LED screen technology — that replaces static printed name tags. E-badges connect via Bluetooth to a smartphone app or web console, allowing instant, remote content updates. The Beambox Nikko features a full-color LED display with NFC and QR code support; the Beambox Nano uses e-ink for extended battery life and lightweight comfort.

Why E-Badges Are Replacing Paper Badges

## FAQ ### What is an electronic badge? An electronic badge is a programmable wearable display device — typically using e-ink or LED screen technology — that shows a person's name, title, credentials, QR codes, and other information dynamically. Unlike printed badges, e-badges can be updated remotely via app or web console. ### How do QR codes work on electronic badges? Electronic badges with QR code display — such as the Beambox Nikko — can generate and display any URL, vCard, WiFi configuration, or plain text. Scanning the QR code with a smartphone allows instant access to the displayed content, enabling paperless networking and lead capture. ### Can electronic badges be updated remotely? Yes. Modern e-badges like Beambox Nikko and Beambox Nano connect via Bluetooth to a smartphone app or web dashboard, allowing content to be updated in real time from anywhere — no physical badge replacement needed. ### What is NFC tap exchange on an electronic badge? NFC (Near Field Communication) tap exchange allows two-badge wearers to share contact information by tapping their badges together. Beambox Nikko includes NFC, enabling instant vCard or profile exchange without scanning a QR code. ### How long does the battery last on an e-badge? Battery life varies by model and usage. Beambox Nano (e-ink) lasts up to 2–3 weeks on a single charge with regular use. Beambox Nikko (LED) provides 8–12 hours of continuous display on a single charge, suitable for full-day events. ### Are electronic badges reusable? Yes. E-badges are designed for thousands of reuse cycles across multiple events. The initial hardware cost is amortized over many uses, making them significantly cheaper per-use than printed badges.
