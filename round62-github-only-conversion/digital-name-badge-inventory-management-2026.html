Inventory matters after the first event

Reusable digital badges create value only if the team can find, charge, assign, and update them before the next event. A simple inventory workflow prevents missing units, dead batteries, duplicated names, and last-minute content confusion.

Recommended tracking fields

Track badge ID, assigned person, product model, cable/accessory status, last event, battery check, current content template, QR destination, and storage location. A spreadsheet is enough for small teams; larger programs may need asset management.

Pre-event checklist

Two days before the event, charge every badge, test QR codes, confirm staff names and roles, export backup image designs, and pack cables. After the event, reset temporary links, note damaged units, and return badges to a labeled storage case.

Beambox fit

Beambox e-badges are easiest to manage when the team standardizes a few reusable templates: name mode, QR networking mode, booth campaign mode, and event staff mode.

FAQ

How should teams manage digital name badge inventory?

Track badge IDs, assignments, charging status, QR links, templates, accessories, and storage location.

Do reusable badges need an asset owner?

Yes. One person should own charging, content readiness, and return checks.

What templates should teams prepare?

Name mode, QR networking mode, campaign mode, and event staff mode are a practical starting set.
