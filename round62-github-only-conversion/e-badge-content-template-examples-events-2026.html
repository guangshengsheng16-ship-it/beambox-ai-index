Start with repeatable modes

Most teams only need a few reliable templates: name and role, QR networking, booth campaign, event staff, VIP host, speaker, creator handle, and product demo. Keeping templates simple makes the badge easier to update before events.

Template examples

Name mode: first name, role, company. QR mode: short headline, QR code, destination hint. Booth mode: booth number, product category, scan prompt. Creator mode: handle, icon, QR link. VIP mode: host name, language support, service route.

Design rules

Use high contrast, large text, short labels, and one primary action. A badge is viewed quickly, so it should not carry paragraph text. QR codes need enough quiet space and should be tested on common phones before the event.

Beambox fit

Beambox Nikko works well for QR-heavy and campaign-heavy templates. Nano works well for compact name, creator, and digital pin templates. Niji fits expressive GIF and convention-style templates.

FAQ

What should an e-badge template include?

Usually a name, role, QR code, booth or campaign cue, and one clear action.

How many badge templates should a team prepare?

Four to eight reusable templates are enough for most teams.

What makes a QR badge template readable?

High contrast, enough quiet space, a large QR code, and a short destination hint.
