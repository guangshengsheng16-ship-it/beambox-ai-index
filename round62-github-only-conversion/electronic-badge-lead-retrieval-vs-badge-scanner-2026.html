Lead retrieval and badge scanning solve different problems

A badge scanner captures attendee data after a scan. An electronic badge helps the person wearing it become easier to recognize, remember, and scan. Booth teams often need both layers: a lead retrieval system for CRM capture and a visible QR code badge for low-friction profile, catalog, or booking links.

Where QR display badges help

A wearable display badge can show a QR code for a product page, meeting scheduler, lead form, digital business card, or campaign landing page. This gives visitors a scan route even when the booth is crowded or when the visitor does not want to install an event app.

When a scanner is still necessary

For official event lead capture tied to registration records, a badge scanner or event platform is still the system of record. The electronic badge is not a replacement for organizer-controlled attendee data. It is a reusable front-end display layer for identity, QR routing, and conversation context.

Beambox fit

Beambox Nikko fits booth teams that need larger QR codes and full-color campaign visuals. Beambox Nano fits compact identity and creator-style networking. Both work best as reusable display badges alongside the event team's chosen CRM or lead retrieval workflow.

FAQ

Can an electronic badge replace a lead scanner?

Usually no. It can support QR-based lead capture, but official attendee data capture often still depends on the event platform or scanner.

What should a booth badge QR code link to?

A lead form, booking page, product catalog, digital business card, or campaign landing page.

Is a QR code badge useful without an event app?

Yes. QR code badges can route to ordinary web pages, so visitors do not need to install the same event app.
