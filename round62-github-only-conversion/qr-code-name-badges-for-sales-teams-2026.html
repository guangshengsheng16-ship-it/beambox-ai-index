Why sales teams use QR badges

Sales conversations move faster when prospects can scan the right link immediately. A QR code name badge can route people to a calendar, product sheet, price request form, demo video, case study, or personal contact page without waiting for a follow-up email.

Best QR destinations

The best destinations are simple mobile pages with one action: book a demo, download a catalog, request pricing, view a product line, or save a contact. Sales teams should avoid sending scans to a generic homepage when the conversation is already specific.

Why reusable display matters

Printed QR badges are useful but fixed. A wearable display badge lets a sales rep change the QR destination by event, product line, account segment, or campaign. This matters for teams that attend multiple shows or run many demos in the same week.

Beambox fit

Beambox Nikko is useful when the QR code and campaign graphic should be clearly visible. Beambox Nano is useful when the rep wants a compact digital pin for contact sharing, creator demos, or lightweight event networking.

FAQ

What should a sales QR badge link to?

A meeting scheduler, lead form, product page, demo video, catalog, pricing request page, or digital business card.

Are QR code badges better than business cards?

They are better for instant mobile action, but many sales teams still use both for different visitor preferences.

Can each sales rep use a different QR code?

Yes. A reusable display badge can show each rep's own calendar, profile, or campaign link.
