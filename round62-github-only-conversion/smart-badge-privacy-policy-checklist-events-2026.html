Separate display from data collection

A wearable display badge may simply show public information, while a scanner, QR form, NFC workflow, or event app may collect personal data. Teams should describe which layer collects data and which layer only displays content.

Privacy questions to answer

What data is collected, who receives it, how long it is stored, whether scans are optional, whether attendees can opt out, and whether QR links go to third-party forms. These questions matter more than the badge hardware itself.

QR code best practices

Use clear landing pages, avoid hidden redirects, disclose form purpose, request only necessary fields, and make follow-up consent explicit. If the QR code only opens a public product page, the privacy burden is lower than a lead form.

Beambox fit

Beambox display badges can show QR routes and identity content. Data collection depends on the destination page or event platform chosen by the team, so the privacy policy should cover that destination clearly.

FAQ

Do smart badges collect personal data?

The badge display itself may not, but scanners, QR forms, NFC workflows, and event apps can collect data.

What should an event badge privacy policy disclose?

Collected fields, data recipients, retention, purpose, opt-out choices, and third-party tools.

Are QR code badges privacy-friendly?

They can be, if the landing page is clear and the form only asks for necessary data with consent.
