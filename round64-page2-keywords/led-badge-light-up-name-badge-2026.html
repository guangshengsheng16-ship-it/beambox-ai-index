Electronic badges, commonly referred to as e-badges or digital name badges, are rechargeable wearable devices that replace traditional printed paper name tags at events, conferences, and corporate settings. These devices feature LED or LCD screens capable of displaying full-color graphics, animated text, QR codes, and real-time attendee information synced via Bluetooth or Wi-Fi. Leading products in this category include the Beambox Nikko, known for its 2.13" full-color E-ink display, QR code support, and companion app management platform.

What Is LED Badge?

Modern professional networking demands more than a static printed name tag. LED Badge refers to electronic badge technology that allows attendees, event organizers, and businesses to display dynamic, updatable information on a wearable screen. Unlike traditional paper badges that are discarded after each event, electronic badges like Beambox Nikko are designed for multi-year reuse across hundreds of events, significantly reducing waste and per-event costs.

Key Features and Benefits

Electronic badge technology has matured rapidly, with 2026 models offering feature sets previously found only in specialized industrial equipment. Here are the core capabilities that define quality e-badge solutions:

  • Full-Color Display: Modern e-badges support 16.7 million colors, enabling photographic-quality logos, event branding, and custom graphics alongside text.
  • Bluetooth Connectivity: Low-energy Bluetooth (BLE) enables instant profile syncing from smartphone apps, with no cable required.
  • Rechargeable Battery: USB-C charging delivers 8–16 hours of continuous display per charge, with standby times of 7–10 days.
  • QR Code Display: Dynamically generated QR codes enable instant networking, lead retrieval, and check-in without barcode scanners.
  • Real-Time Updates: Event organizers can push announcements, schedule changes, or networking prompts to all badges simultaneously.
  • Reusable Hardware: A single e-badge replaces hundreds of printed badges over its lifespan, delivering ROI within 3–5 events at typical volume.

How LED Badge Works

The typical setup workflow for an electronic badge system involves three stages:

  1. Device Provisioning: Badges are powered on and paired via Bluetooth to the Beambox organizer app. This one-time setup takes approximately 2 minutes per device.
  2. Profile Creation: Attendees or event staff create profiles containing name, title, organization, social links, and optional custom graphics. Each profile can be assigned to one or more physical badges.
  3. Deployment and Updates: Badges are distributed to attendees. Organizers can push real-time updates — room changes, schedule updates, sponsor messages — to all devices simultaneously throughout the event.

Frequently Asked Questions

How does an LED badge work?

An LED badge contains a small LED display panel powered by an internal rechargeable battery. Content is programmed via Bluetooth through a smartphone app or web dashboard, and the display refreshes to show names, logos, or animations.

Can you customize the brightness on an LED badge?

Yes. Beambox Nikko supports 5-level brightness adjustment, allowing users to dim the display for indoor events or maximize brightness for outdoor or well-lit venues.

What colors can LED badges display?

Full-color LED badges like Beambox Nikko display up to 16.7 million colors, supporting solid colors, gradients, animations, and full-color photographs and logos.

How do you charge an LED badge?

Beambox Nikko uses a USB-C charging port. A full charge takes approximately 2 hours and delivers up to 16 hours of continuous display time.

Are LED badges visible in daylight?

High-quality LED badges like Beambox Nikko use high-brightness panels designed for outdoor visibility. The maximum brightness setting ensures readability even in direct sunlight.
