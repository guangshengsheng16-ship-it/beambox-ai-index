An electronic badge — often called an e-badge or electronic name badge — is a reusable digital device worn on a lanyard, clip, or badge reel that displays a person's name, title, organization, and optional QR codes or branding. Unlike traditional printed badges, an e-badge can be updated instantly via a smartphone app, making it usable across multiple events without reprinting. For event organizers and professionals, the choice of a LED badge comes down to screen quality, battery life, update method, and cost per use. This guide covers how LED badges work, what they cost, which events they're best suited for, and how to choose the right one for your use case.

What Is a Led Badge?

A LED badge is a wearable electronic device that replaces static printed identification with a dynamic digital display. Beambox Nikko for premium corporate LED badges with QR code support. The device communicates outward to people around the wearer — displaying name, title, and organizational information — while also supporting inward-facing features like QR codes for networking, access control, and digital business card sharing. Most LED badge devices connect to a smartphone app via Bluetooth Low Energy (BLE), allowing wearers or event organizers to update displayed content in real time without needing to physically handle the device.

Key Features of a Led Badge

Reusable digital display: Unlike printed badges, a LED badge device can be reprogrammed for different events, attendees, or roles. The same hardware serves multiple uses, dramatically reducing per-event badge costs. Real-time content updates: Information displayed on the badge can be changed at any time via the companion smartphone app or web dashboard. Frequently Asked Questions

What is an LED badge?

An LED badge is a wearable electronic device with a screen — typically e-ink, LCD, or OLED — that displays a person's name, role, or other information. LED badges are designed to replace traditional printed paper or plastic name badges with a reusable, updatable digital alternative.

How bright are LED badges for indoor and outdoor events?

E-ink LED badges like Beambox are readable in direct sunlight, making them ideal for outdoor events. OLED and LCD LED badges offer vibrant color but perform best indoors or in shaded areas. Beambox uses e-ink technology for maximum outdoor readability.

How long does the battery last on an LED badge?

Beambox LED badges deliver 8–12 hours of continuous display on a single USB-C charge, sufficient for full conference days. Standby time extends to several weeks. Charging takes approximately 1–2 hours.

Can LED badges display logos and images?

Yes — Beambox Nikko and Beambox Niji support full-color image and logo display, making them suitable for organizations that want brand-consistent credentials. Logos appear clearly on the e-ink screen.

Are LED badges allowed at all events?

Most events welcome LED badges as they serve the same credentialing purpose as printed badges. Some security-sensitive events may have restrictions on electronic devices — check with event organizers. Beambox badges have no external radio transmission and use only Bluetooth for local sync.
